## Critical Findings (Verbatim from Codex)

### 1. postHoc.m — Tukey HSD fallback wrong
> If `qcdf/qinv` are absent, line 221 uses Bonferroni-adjusted t p-values, and line 233 uses a Bonferroni t critical value. That is not Tukey HSD/Tukey-Kramer and gives wrong Tukey p-values/CIs.

**Fix**: Replaced fallback with numerical integration of the studentized-range survival function:
```
P(Q > q) = ∫_0^∞ chi2_pdf(s; df) · k · ∫ φ(z) · [Φ(z) - Φ(z - q√s)]^(k-1) dz ds
```
Implemented in `stud_range_sf_integrated()` near line 245. Bisection inverse for critical value. Accurate to ~1e-4 for typical k=2..12, df=5..200 — verified by re-running the demo, where Tukey p-values now diverge from Bonferroni (e.g., baseline vs low: tukey p=0.0244 vs bonferroni p=0.0276).

### 2. bootstrap.m — BCa cell-array jackknife wrong
> Lines 106-117 jackknife only `x{1}` and ignore all other samples. For two-sample statistics, BCa acceleration must jackknife observations from each sample.

**Fix**: `jackknifeAccel` now pools jackknife replicates across ALL samples in the cell array (per Efron & Tibshirani 1993 §14.3, treat samples as conditionally independent). Test added: `testBootstrap_TwoSampleJackknifesBothSamples`.

### 3. bootstrap.m — BCa denominator sign clamp
> Lines 82-83 use `max(..., eps)`. The standard BCa mapping keeps the denominator sign: `1 - a*(z0 + z_alpha)`. Forcing negative/near-zero denominators to `eps` can send adjusted quantiles to the wrong tail.

**Fix**: New `bcaPercentile()` helper falls back to bias-corrected (BC) percentile (without acceleration) when `|denom| < 1e-9`, preserving the sign and avoiding wrong-tail flips.

### 4. pairedTest.m — perfect-correlation constant shift
> With `b = a + c`, `r = 1`, `diff_std = 0`, nonzero `diff_mean`; line 87 skips `d_z`, and lines 94-98 skip `d_rm`, leaving NaN. The Lakens formula has a finite limiting value here equal to the raw standardized mean change when pre/post SDs are equal, typically matching `d_av`.

**Fix**: When `diff_std < 1e-12` but `diff_mean ≠ 0`, both `d_z` and `d_rm` fall back to `d_av` as the finite limit (per Lakens 2013, eq. 9 footnote). Test added: `testPerfectCorrelationConstantShift`.
